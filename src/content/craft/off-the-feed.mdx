There's a version of running a studio that lives mostly inside someone else's
feed. Daily threads, weekly retros in public, hot takes for engagement, a
constant low hum of *here is the thing I am about to do, here is the thing I
am doing, here is the thing I just did.*

We don't begrudge anyone who works this way — some people genuinely think
better in public. But we've found the opposite: that the part of us that
wants to post is, almost always, the part that should be writing code, calling
a friend, or going outside.
