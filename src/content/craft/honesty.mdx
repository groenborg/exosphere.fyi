The most useful thing a consultant can do, most of the time, is tell their
client something the rest of the org has been carefully avoiding saying out
loud. The price of doing this well is the willingness to occasionally be
fired for it.

We've been fired for it. We'd do it again. The alternative — taking the money
and quietly building the thing we know won't work — is the kind of trade that
catches up with you, and that we'd rather not make.
