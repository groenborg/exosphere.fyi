A studio is, structurally, an exercise in trust. Two people have to trust
each other enough to make decisions without consulting. Clients have to trust
that the bill reflects work that needed doing. The people at home have to
trust that the laptop will close at some point.

You can't have one of those without the others. A team that's wonderful to
colleagues but absent at home isn't a healthy team — it's a debt about to come
due. We try to keep all three accounts in good standing, and treat any one of
them slipping as the early-warning signal it usually is.
