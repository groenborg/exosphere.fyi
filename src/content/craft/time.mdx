The most expensive thing in any project isn't the engineers, the
infrastructure, or the licensing. It's the time spent doing the wrong work
attentively.

We don't bill hours because the hourly model rewards exactly that — the slow,
careful production of the thing that shouldn't have been made. We'd rather
charge for an outcome, ship it in a week, and disappear than charge for three
months of motion.

The same logic applies in reverse: we won't take your time for things that
should be a paragraph in a doc.
