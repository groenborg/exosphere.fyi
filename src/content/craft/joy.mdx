The hustle framing — *grind now, live later* — has a body count. We've watched
enough talented people burn out before forty to be done pretending it's a
serious philosophy.

The alternative isn't laziness. It's the deliberate refusal to treat work and
life as a zero-sum trade. People who enjoy their lives produce better work,
on average, than people who don't. People who enjoy their work go home in a
better mood. The compounding is real, in both directions.
