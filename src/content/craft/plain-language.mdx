Corporate language exists to soften, hedge, and diffuse responsibility. It is
extremely good at its job. It is also the single biggest tax on the speed and
honesty of a working team.

We try to write the way we'd talk to a smart friend who hadn't been in the
meeting. *We were late because the API was broken.* Not *delivery encountered
upstream-dependency-related velocity headwinds.* It's not a style preference;
it's a thing that changes how decisions get made.
