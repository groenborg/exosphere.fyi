The default move in most companies — *let's get on a call* — is a tax paid by
the people who'd otherwise be doing the work. It's the lowest-effort thing for
the requester and the most expensive thing for everyone else.

We start the other way around. If something can be written, write it. If a
written exchange would take a week, *then* a thirty-minute call is a saving,
not a cost. The question is never "should we meet" but "what's the cheapest
shape for resolving this".
