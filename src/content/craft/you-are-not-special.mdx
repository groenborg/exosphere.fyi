Tyler Durden's gift to working teams. The single most useful frame we've
found for criticism that lands, and feedback that doesn't sting: leave the
sense of yourself as a special, gifted, deserving thing at the door.

This sounds harsh. In practice it's a kindness. People who've quietly
accepted they aren't special can hear *this is bad* without flinching. They
can say *I was wrong* without it costing them anything internally. They can
let go of a bad idea before it becomes an identity.

The opposite — protecting the precious self — is what produces every
untouchable bad idea, every silent grudge after a code review, every
meeting where the obvious goes unsaid because saying it would mean saying
it to someone *important*. We've worked in those teams. We're done.
