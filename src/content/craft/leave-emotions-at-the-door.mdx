The most useful sentence in a working partnership is *I think this is wrong*.
The second most useful is *you might be right*. Both are almost impossible to
say honestly if the work and the person making it are tangled up in the
listener's head.

So we practice the separation. The draft is not the writer. The
implementation is not the engineer. The pitch is not the partner. Criticism
of the work product is information — sometimes uncomfortable information —
but it's not a verdict on the human delivering it.

This is harder than it sounds, and we don't always get it right. The
discipline isn't *don't feel anything*. It's *don't let what you feel about
the work and what you feel about the person ride in the same vehicle*. It
only works if the trust underneath is real, which is why it sits right next
to the trust essay on this list.
